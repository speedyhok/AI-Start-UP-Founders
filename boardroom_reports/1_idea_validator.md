**To:** Investment Committee

**Subject:** Viability Analysis for "CurveStream" (Niche Adult Media SaaS)

---

## 1. Executive Summary
CurveStream aims to disrupt the fragmented adult entertainment market by providing a high-fidelity, community-driven platform exclusively for the plus-size demographic. While the adult industry is often dismissed by institutional capital, the "niche-subscription" model currently commands some of the highest ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) metrics in the digital economy.

## 2. Market Sizing (The Opportunity)
The global adult content market is shifting from legacy tube sites to creator-led subscription models.

*   **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** $18.5 Billion. This encompasses the total global spend on adult digital entertainment, including premium subscriptions, tips, and pay-per-view content.
*   **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):** $2.1 Billion. This represents the "body-positive/niche" segment of the adult industry, which has shown a 22% YOY growth in search volume for "plus-size" and "curvy" categories.
*   **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** $32 Million. Based on a Year 3 target of capturing 1.5% of the SAM through targeted influencer marketing and SEO dominance, aiming for 250,000 active monthly subscribers at an average net revenue of $10.60/user/month.

## 3. Competitive Landscape
The market is currently underserved by generic platforms that fail to curate content effectively.

1.  **OnlyFans:** The market leader. High liquidity, but poor discoverability for niche categories. Users often get "lost in the noise."
2.  **ManyVids:** A legacy platform with a strong plus-size category, but suffers from high transaction fees (up to 40%) and an outdated UI/UX that lacks mobile-first optimization.
3.  **Fansly:** The primary challenger to OnlyFans. Offers better tagging, but lacks a brand identity focused on the plus-size community, leading to lower conversion rates for specific body-type searches.

## 4. Growth Projections
We are projecting a **115% YoY CAGR** over the first 36 months, driven by viral marketing on TikTok/Instagram (via body-positivity influencers) and a proprietary recommendation algorithm.

*   **Year 1:** $4.2M Revenue (User Acquisition Phase)
*   **Year 2:** $12.8M Revenue (Community & Retention Phase)
*   **Year 3:** $32.0M Revenue (Scale & Monetization Phase)

## 5. SWOT Analysis
*   **Strengths:** High brand loyalty, untapped niche community, lower CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) due to highly targeted social media advertising.
*   **Weaknesses:** High reliance on third-party payment processors (Stripe/CCBill), potential platform risk if policies change.
*   **Opportunities:** Expansion into physical merchandising and live-stream events; potential for "body-positive" brand partnerships.
*   **Threats:** Regulatory crackdowns on adult content hosting; "shadowbanning" by mainstream social media platforms for marketing.

**Risk Rating: 7/10** (High regulatory/payment risk balanced by high demand).

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
1.  **CAC to LTV Ratio:** Target > 1:4.
2.  **Churn Rate:** Target < 6% monthly.
3.  **Content Creator Density:** Aiming for 5,000+ verified creators by the end of Year 1.

## 7. Founders Action Item
**Research Target:** Conduct a "Payment Processor Stress Test." Identify and interview three alternative high-risk merchant account providers (e.g., SegPay, Epoch, or RocketGate) to build a multi-redundant payment architecture. This is the single greatest point of failure for your business model. Do not proceed until you have a signed LOI from at least two secondary processors.
